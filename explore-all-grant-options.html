The Fully Supported Journey

For large-scale projects, the One-Stop-Shop service provides end-to-end support. We manage everything from assessment and grant applications to contractor selection and quality assurance, ensuring a hassle-free journey to a warmer home.

Pros:

  • Convenience: Single point of contact.
  • Comprehensive Upgrades: Multiple upgrades together.
  • Grant Management: Handled by provider.
  • Upfront Grant Deduction: Often available.

Cons:

  • Less Flexibility: On contractors/materials.
  • Potentially Higher Cost: Due to management fees.
  • Commitment: Best for large-scale retrofits.

What is the scale of your planned upgrade?

This path is ideal for large-scale, complex retrofits involving multiple measures at once (e.g., insulation, windows, and a heat pump).

How much time and effort are you willing to put into managing the project?

Choose this path if you prefer a 'hands-off' approach where a single company manages all contractors, timelines, and paperwork for you.

About the One Stop Shop Path

A One-Stop-Shop offers a fully managed, end-to-end service. They handle everything from the initial assessment, grant application, contractor management, to quality assurance. Your home's existing BERBER stands for Building Energy Rating. It's like an energy label for your home (A-G), showing its energy efficiency. A-rated is most efficient. must be B3 or lower. About the Community Grant Initiative

Warm Home Hub also facilitates group applications by partnering with an approved One-Stop-Shop. This allows a community of homeowners to undertake upgrades together, potentially leading to economies of scale and shared learning. It's the same fully managed process, but for a group.

The Self-Managed Journey

If you prefer to manage your own project or want to upgrade your home in stages, the individual grant path is for you. We provide the guidance and information you need to make informed decisions and find registered contractors.

Pros:

  • Flexibility: Choose upgrades and timing.
  • Control: Select your own contractors.
  • Paced Investment: Spread cost over time.

Cons:

  • More Effort: You manage the full project.
  • Coordination: Can be complex.
  • Grant Paid Post-Works: Pay upfront, then claim.

What is the scale of your planned upgrade?

This path is perfect if you plan to tackle one or two specific upgrades at a time, or want to phase your upgrades over a longer period.

How much time and effort are you willing to put into managing the project?

Choose this path if you are comfortable managing contractors, timelines, and grant applications yourself. It requires a more 'hands-on' approach.

About the DIY Path

This path allows you to apply for grants for specific, individual energy upgrades (e.g., attic insulation). You manage the project yourself, including choosing registered contractors and applying for grants for each measure. Build year cutoffs apply (e.g., pre-2011 for insulation, pre-2021 for renewables).
