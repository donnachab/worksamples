Strategy Guide

How to Get What You Want From Your AI

A Strategic Framework for Generative AI.

The Problem

Why does it keep repeating mistakes?

You give clear instructions. The AI ignores them. You repeat yourself. It apologizes, then does the exact same thing again.

It feels like stubbornness. It's actually a memory failure.

The Mechanism

The Context Bucket

Every AI has a "Context Window." Think of it as a bucket.

Every word you type goes into the bucket. When the bucket is full, the oldest data blocks spill out to make room for the new.

New Concept

It's a Prediction Engine

Unlike a database, AI doesn't "know" facts. It statistically predicts the next likely word.

System: Generating output based on highest probability tokens...

The Consequence

Forgotten Instructions

If your "Rules" were at the start of a long chat, they might have fallen out of the bucket. The AI no longer knows those rules exist.

Quick Fixes

How to clear the noise and get back on track.

Tool #1

The "Fresh Start"

If the AI is looping or confused, don't argue. Arguing fills the bucket with bad data.

"Ignore previous instructions."
"Start a new task:"
"Focus ONLY on the following:"

Tool #2

Use Structured Checklists

The Paragraph (Bad)

"I also noticed you used asterisks again, which I said not to do, and you forgot the inventory slide, can you fix that?"

The Checklist (Good)
  • ✓ Remove all asterisks.
  • ✓ Add the 'Inventory' slide.

Tool #3

One Task at a Time

Don't ask for a summary, a code rewrite, and a joke in one prompt. Split complex requests.

Setting the Stage

Defining the AI's persona.

Concept

The "Job Description"

Don't just start chatting. Give the AI a title. This primes its prediction engine to use vocabulary from that profession.

"You are an expert Senior Python Architect."

Knowledge vs. Attitude

The Role

What it knows.

"Senior Editor"

The Personality

How it speaks.

"Be direct and concise."

Efficiency Hack

Kill the Politeness

AI apologies waste memory (tokens) and fill up your context bucket with fluff. Tell the system to stop.

Your Prompt: "Do not apologize. Do not explain what you did wrong. Just output the corrected text."

Strategy

The Perfect Colleague

You want the Role of an expert, with the Attitude of a subordinate.

Pro Tips

Advanced steering techniques.

Tip #1

Give Examples (Few-Shot)

Don't just describe the format. Show it. Engineers call this "Few-Shot Prompting."

Input: "Apple" -> Output: "Fruit: Red"
Input: "Spinach" -> Output: "Vegetable: Green"

Tip #2

"Show Your Work"

If you ask for a complex answer immediately, the AI guesses. If you ask it to "think step-by-step," it reasons.

Tip #3

Provide Source Material

Connect the AI brain to a Reference Library. Engineers call this RAG.

It forces the system to read facts rather than predicting them.

"Answer using ONLY the text provided below."

Tip #4

Preventing "Drift"

As the conversation gets long, the original instructions are far away. Remind the AI of its role every 5-10 messages.

Tip #5

The Pattern Break

If the AI starts hallucinating, STOP immediately. Use a "Stop Protocol" to force it to re-verify facts.

Rule: "STOP. You are hallucinating facts not in evidence."

Conclusion

You are the Pilot

The AI is a powerful engine, but it has no steering wheel. You are the steering wheel.

Pre-Flight Checklist

  • ✓ Define Role: "You are an Expert..."
  • ✓ Define Task: "Your goal is to..."
  • ✓ Constraints: "No emojis. Be brief."

Safe Flying.
