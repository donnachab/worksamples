How to Get What You Want From Your AI

A non-technical guide to stopping loops and getting better answers.

The Problem

Getting Stuck

Have you ever felt like the AI is ignoring your latest instructions and keeps repeating mistakes? It feels like it's looping.

This isn't a "bug" or stubbornness—it's a memory issue.

The Cause

Short-Term Memory

The AI has a limited "Context Window." Think of it like a small bucket for our conversation.

When the bucket is full, the oldest parts of our chat spill out and are forgotten forever.

Mechanics

How Memory Works

Imagine a transparent box. New messages enter from the right, pushing old messages off the left edge.

If your original instructions fall off the edge, the AI literally doesn't know they exist anymore.

Part 1

Quick Fixes

Simple tricks to get back on track when things go wrong.

Tool #1

The "Fresh Start"

Sometimes you need to clear the desk. Tell the AI to ignore the mess and focus on right now.

"New task:"
"Ignore previous instructions."
"Focus ONLY on this:"
Why this works:

It tells the AI that the old conversation is clutter and to treat your new request as the only thing that matters.

Tool #2: Use Checklists

Paragraphs are easy to misread. Numbered lists are like a recipe—easy to follow step-by-step.

Hard to Follow

"...I also see you're using asterisks again, and you forgot the inventory, and the tone is wrong..."

Easy to Follow

Please fix these 2 things:

  1. Stop using asterisks.
  2. Add the 'Inventory' slide.

Tool #3

One Task at a Time

Don't ask for a summary, a code rewrite, and a joke all at once.

Why this works:

Just like humans, AI does better quality work when it focuses on one single job at a time.

Part 2

Setting the Stage

How to set up the AI to succeed before you even start.

Concept

The "Job Description"

Don't just start chatting. You are in the driver's seat. Give the AI a job title and a rulebook first.

This is a Master Prompt. It stops the AI from guessing what you want and tells it exactly who to be.

Knowledge vs. Attitude

Role (Knowledge)

What the AI knows.

"You are an expert Senior Editor."
"You are a Python Architect."

Personality (Attitude)

How the AI speaks.

"Be brief. No fluff."
"Explain it like I'm 5 years old."

Example: Stop the Apologies

Default (Annoying)

"I am so, so sorry. I apologize for the confusion. I will try to do better next time..."

With Instructions

[Rule: Do not apologize. Just fix the error.]

"Understood. Here is the corrected code."

Strategy

The Perfect Colleague

You can combine these to create the perfect helper. You get the smarts of an expert with the efficiency of a pro.

The Prompt:

"You are a Senior Writer. Be direct. Don't use jargon."


Result: High quality work, zero wasted time.

Part 3

Pro Tips

Advanced tricks to guide the AI's thinking.

Tip #1

Give Examples

Don't just describe what you want. Show an example. It's the fastest way to get the right format.

Example 1: Input: "Apple" Output: "Fruit"
Example 2: Input: "Carrot" Output: "Vegetable"
Your task: Input: "Spinach" Output: ...

Tip #2

"Show Your Work"

If you ask for a complex answer immediately, the AI might guess. Ask it to think step-by-step first.

"Think step-by-step and explain your logic before giving the final answer."
Why this works: It forces the AI to slow down and check its own logic, reducing mistakes.

Tip #3

Stick to the Facts

AI can make things up ("hallucinate") if it doesn't know the answer. Prevent this by giving it the source material.

"Use ONLY the text I paste below to answer my question. Do not invent new facts."
