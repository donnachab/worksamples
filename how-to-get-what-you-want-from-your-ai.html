How to Get What You Want From Your AI

A non-technical guide to stopping loops and getting better answers.

The Problem

Getting Stuck

Have you ever felt like the AI is ignoring your latest instructions and keeps repeating mistakes? It feels like it's looping.

This isn't a "bug" or stubbornnessâ€”it's a memory issue.

The Cause

Short-Term Memory

The AI has a limited "Context Window." Think of it like a small bucket for our conversation.

When the bucket is full, the oldest parts of our chat spill out and are forgotten forever.

Mechanics

How Memory Works

Imagine a box. New messages enter from the left, pushing old messages off the right edge.

If your original instructions fall off the edge, the AI literally doesn't know they exist anymore.

Part 1: Quick Fixes

Simple tricks to get back on track.

Tool #1

The "Fresh Start"

Sometimes you need to clear the desk. Tell the AI to ignore the mess and focus on right now.

"New task:" "Ignore previous instructions." "Focus ONLY on this:"

Tool #2

Use Checklists

Paragraphs are easy to misread. Numbered lists are clearer.

Hard to Follow

"...I also see you're using asterisks again, and you forgot the inventory..."

Easy to Follow

Please fix these 2 things:

  1. Stop using asterisks.
  2. Add the 'Inventory' slide.
Why this works

Structured formats reduce the chance of misinterpretation.

Tool #3

One Task at a Time

Don't ask for a summary, a code rewrite, and a joke all at once.

Why this works

Just like humans, AI does better quality work when it focuses on one single job at a time.

Part 2: Setting the Stage

Concept

The "Job Description"

Don't just start chatting. Give the AI a job title (Master Prompt).

Knowledge vs. Attitude

Role (Knowledge)

What the AI knows.

"You are an expert Senior Editor."
"You are a Python Architect."

Personality (Attitude)

How the AI speaks.

"Be brief. No fluff."
"Explain it like I'm 5 years old."

Example: Stop the Apologies

Default (Annoying)

"I am so, so sorry. I apologize for the confusion. I will try to do better next time..."

With Instructions

[Rule: Do not apologize. Just fix the error.]

"Understood. Here is the corrected code."

Strategy

The Perfect Colleague

Combine smarts and efficiency.

Part 3: Pro Tips

Tip #1

Give Examples

Example 1: Input: "Apple" -> Output: "Fruit" Input: "Spinach" -> Output: ...

Tip #2

"Show Your Work"

Ask it to think step-by-step first.

Tip #3

Stick to the Facts

Prevent hallucinations by providing source material.

Tip #4

Preventing "Drift"

Re-anchor to the source of truth often.

Tip #5

The Pattern Break

Stop it immediately when it hallucinates.

"STOP. Review my previous message."
